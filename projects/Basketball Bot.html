Basketball Robot Summary

This is a project for my Mechatronics class at Purdue University. The final project involved creating a robot that could should baskets autonomously. The final results are below in three different matches. Each match is three minutes long and pins two robots against each other. Which ever robot scores more points wins. More details on the rules of the matches can be found in the competition description section. The end result was our robot demolishing the others. Our robot could reliably make four to six shoots from half court. No other robot was able to make more than one shot into the basket.

Potential Improvements

There are many improvements that could be made. The most pressing issue was our ball feeding ramp had issues with more than four balls at a time. This could be solved with a better filter device rather than just a vibrating motor. By fixing this, the time spent returning home for more balls could be spent shooting. It would likely double our shots in a three minutes. Another improvement could be putting an encoder on the shooter motor. The shots were not precise. Throttling in the shooter motor is likely at fault. Being able to decern how much variation in rpm leads to variation in accuracy would be a start. Lastly, wheels and motors could be replaced with similar higher quality parts. The motors had trouble moving against friction side ways. If we had better mecanums or stronger motors this would not have been a problem. However, budget was an issue, so buying four replacement motors was not possible.
